Survey and Focus Group Report

In order to better understand the feelings and perspectives of the client's retired employees, our team conducted a survey as well as a focus group on the topic of pension statements and how to best deliver information to retired employees in the future.

The client was keen to have a clear understanding of how the current publications, as well as the design, production and delivery of the annual pension statement were serving the retiree population. In order to explore the possibility of an online portal in the future, we also included questions to collect opinions and preferences around changes in the delivery of information via different technologies.

This report was written and prepared by me using the information collected from the survey and focus group meeting.

Total Rewards and Pension Statement Projects

For all statement projects featured here, I have managed the full construction of the statement template and specifications documents. I also oversaw the entire process of production and distribution through to completion, directing a team of colleagues as well as external vendors. For many of these clients, the statement project recurred over multiple years.

Writer, Project Manager

For this Total Rewards Statement project, the client had never before created a Total Rewards statement; I built the specifications document, corresponding data, and content from their pension and benefits package. The design template was created from scratch based on our own research and analysis of statement design.

The statement is made up of three main parts: on the front page is the header on the top half, and the five main sections on the bottom half. Specific plan information can be found on the back page.

The header contains the most high-level information, such as the employee's name and their total rewards number. The second section holds more detailed information, breaking that high-level number down into five columns. Each column is colour-coded and assigned an icon for easy, fast identification. For those individuals that are interested in the fine print, the third section on the back page contains the finer details about each benefit.

Editor, Project Manager

For this pension statement, the client had been sending out the statement each year to their employees, however it was a simple Word file with no design features. It was difficult to read, drowning in actuarial pension jargon, and key information was buried.

The first thing to do was to put the most relevant information on the front page: how much money the employee had accumulated. On the second page, the template contains a bright purple box containing important beneficiary and employment information. All other pension information follows after this.

Project Manager

This client had been publishing their Total Rewards Statements for many years, but wanted to move the printed statement into a portal where employees could log in and view a PDF version.

We maintained the concept of the previous layout, with a summary on the first page that allowed the employee to see the total rewards number immediately, and a subsequent page for each section with more detailed information.
