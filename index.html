Quarterly Publication

The 'Boosting your kraft-ability' quarterly magazine was created for the client as an engaging way to communicate the company's Health & Wellness portfolio to employees nationwide in both French and English. In order to reach every employee, whether they be in front of a computer or on the factory floor, I also prepared manager's notes to prompt discussion during team meetings, as well as posters to be hung in Kraft locations across Canada.

As chief editor and project manager, I began each year with an overall schedule for all issues. I prepared article concepts that were then fine-tuned, and myself or another writer would complete the full article. For several issues, I acted as the sole writer, including the Winter edition on the right. Download the PDF for a complete sample of my writing.

In addition to this, I also prepared the layout and worked with the graphic designer to finalize the overall theme for each issue. Once layout and articles were complete, the finished newsletter was sent off to the printer for production and distribution.

Survey and Focus Group Report

In order to better understand the feelings and perspectives of the client's retired employees, our team conducted a survey as well as a focus group on the topic of pension statements and how to best deliver information to retired employees in the future.

The client was keen to have a clear understanding of how the current publications, as well as the design, production and delivery of the annual pension statement were serving the retiree population. In order to explore the possibility of an online portal in the future, we also included questions to collect opinions and preferences around changes in the delivery of information via different technologies.

I wrote and prepared this report using the information collected from the survey, as well as the focus group meeting.

Writer, Project Manager

For this Total Rewards Statement project, the client had never before created a Total Rewards statement; I built the specifications document, corresponding data, and content from their pension and benefits package. The design template was created by our team based on our own research and analysis of Total Reward Statement design.

The statement is made up of three main parts: on the front page is the header at the tope, and the five main sections on the bottom. Specific plan information can be found on the back page.

The header contains the most high-level information, such as the employee's name and their Total Rewards number. The second section holds more detailed information, breaking that high-level number down into five columns. Each column is colour-coded and assigned an icon for easy, fast identification. For those individuals that are interested in the fine print, the third section on the back page contains the finer details about each benefit.

Editor, Project Manager

For this pension statement, the client had been sending out the statement each year to their employees, however it was a simple Word file with no design features. It was difficult to read, drowning in actuarial pension jargon, burying key information that the employee cared most about.

The first thing to do was to put the most relevant information on the front page: how much money the employee had accumulated. On the second page, the template contains a bright purple box containing important beneficiary and employment information. All other pension information follows after this.
